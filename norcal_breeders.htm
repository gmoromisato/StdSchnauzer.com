The below contact information are SSCNC Members who from time to time have litters of puppies or older adults to place.

SSCNC does not endorse one breeder over another on this list and recommends you contact them individually. Although they may not currently have a litter at their home, they will know of other Standard Schnauzer breeders you may be able to contact.

All the listed breeders adhere to the Standard Schnauzer Club of America’s Breeder Ethics which includes health checks and certificates, not selling to puppy brokers or pet stores and maintain the highest quality of health. They also adhere to the Written Breed Standard.

SSCNC highly recommends that all perspective puppy owners meet the Sire and Dam of the litter and talk with breeders before deciding on a Standard Schnauzer—most breeders will be more than willing to share the good and the bad qualities of the breed with you to ensure that a puppy they place has the best quality of life. The best place to meet a Standard Schnauzer is usually at the local dog shows; as most of the Breeders actively show their dogs in Conformation or other companion events.

Lastly, Standard Schnauzers can take some time to acquire—nationally we usually produce less than 75 litters per year so start early with your research and be patient for your next Standard Schnauzer is most likely going to be in your life longer than your current vehicle.
