Legislative Activities

Throughout the United States, the Animal Rights movement is seeking to whittle away our rights of animal ownership. Their cause is to end animal ownership, breeding, consuming and use of animal products by all U.S. citizens.

On the municipal and county levels they seek to change “Owners” to “Guardians” changing the rights of ownership. They also seek to mandate the end of certain breeds by Breed Specific Legislation (BSL) that would outlaw certain breeds. Rather than punishing the acts of a few, they seek to eliminate the breeds’ existence as a whole.

On the State level they invoke mandatory spay and neutering (MSN)—attempting to lump all “breeders” into one category umbrella’d by the vial images of “puppy mills”. They also disregard recent studies showing that early spay and neutering is actually detrimental to the dogs’ overall adult health, temperament, and structure

SSCNC actively monitors and rallies with other all-breed groups, veterinary groups, training clubs, law enforcement and canine assistance groups to fight these overly broad and unconstitutional attempts to revoke our dog owning rights and privileges.
